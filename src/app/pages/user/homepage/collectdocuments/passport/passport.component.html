Passport-style photos

For Forms I-130, I-485, and (Forms I-131 and I-765 if applicable)
The application requires passport-style photos of both {{us.data.application.name || "Applicant"}} and {{us.data.application.sponsorname || "Sponsor"}}, taken within 30 days of filing the application.
On the back of each photo, lightly print the person's name and A-Number (if any) using a pencil or felt pen.
›
Photo requirements

The photos must have a white to off-white background, be printed on thin paper with a glossy finish, and be unmounted and unretouched.

The two recent identical color passport-style photos must be 2 by 2 inches. The photos must be in color with full face, frontal view on a white to off-white background. Head height should measure 1 to 1 3/8 inches from top of hair to bottom of chin, and eye height is between 1 1/8 to 1 3/8 inches from bottom of photo. Your head must be bare unless you are wearing headwear as required by a religious denomination of which you are a member. Visit the Department Of State website for photo examples.

Because of the current USCIS scanning process, if a digital photo is submitted, it must be produced from a high-resolution camera that has at least 3.5 mega pixels of resolution.

Source: USCIS Form I-130 Instructions Page 6, USCIS Form I-485 Instructions Page 7, USCIS Form I-131 Instructions Page 11, USCIS Form I-765 Instructions Page 26

Greenbroad Exclusive Money Saving Tip

If you need multiple copies of the passport-style photos, it can be much cheaper to take the photos by yourselves and then print them out at a photo printing kiosk. Just make sure the photos you take meet the passport-style photo requirements.

Tools for taking passport-style photos:

  • The Photo Tool provided by the U.S. Department of State can crop your photo to the right size.
  • There are also passport photo tools on iPhone and Android.
Source: Greenbroad suggestion, not from USCIS.
