Here are key points for the medical examination for a Marriage Green Card:
    Finding the Right Doctor:
  • Research authorized civil surgeons designated by U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS) in your area.
  • Check the USCIS website or contact the nearest U.S. embassy or consulate for a list of approved doctors.
    • Scheduling the Exam:
  • Contact the chosen civil surgeon's office to schedule the medical examination.
  • Plan the exam well in advance to ensure completion within the required timeframe.
    • Documents to Bring:
  • Valid passport or government-issued identification document.
  • Vaccination records (if available).
  • Any medical documentation relevant to past or current conditions (if applicable).
    • What to Expect at the Exam:
  • The civil surgeon will conduct a thorough physical examination, including vital signs, blood tests, and a review of your medical history.
  • The doctor will assess your overall health and screen for communicable diseases specified by USCIS.
  • Chest X-ray and syphilis testing may be required as part of the examination.
    • Money-Saving Tip:
  • Consider obtaining any required vaccinations before the exam from a lower-cost source (such as a local health department) to potentially save on vaccination fees charged by the civil surgeon.
