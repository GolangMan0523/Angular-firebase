Marriage documents

For Form I-130
The following documents help to establish that {{us.data.application.sponsorname || "Sponsor"}} and {{us.data.application.name && us.data.application.name + "'s" || "Applicant's"}} are married.
If you submit a document with information in a foreign language, you must also submit a full English translation. The translator must sign a certification that the English language translation is complete and accurate, and that he or she is competent to translate from the foreign language into English. The certification must include the translator’s signature. USCIS recommends the certification contain the translator’s printed name and the date and the translator’s contact information.

What if I have a marriage or divorce certificate from another country?

You can find the requirements for an acceptable marriage or divorce certificate from each country using the Department of State Document Finder tool.Select your country from the left-hand-side list in the tool, and then scroll down to the section called "Marriage, Divorce Certificates". Direct links to some popular countries: China, Mexico, Dominican Republic, Philippines, India, Vietnam.

Source: USCIS Form I-864 Page 8

Greenbroad Exclusive Money Saving Tip

You do not need to pay for a professional translation service for document translation. For example, a friend who is competent to translate can satisfy the requirement.

Source: Greenbroad suggestion only, not from USCIS instructions
