    What is a cover letter?

A cover letter is essentially a table of contents of all the forms and documents contained within an application packet. A cover letter helps you organize your papers, and also helps USCIS agents during their review.

For easy processing, USCIS advises the application materials be organized into two separate packets:

  • The Petition for Alien Relative packet ‚Äì used by {{us.data.application.sponsorname || "Sponsor"}} to establish the existence of a marital relationship with {{us.data.application.name || "Applicant"}}
  • The Adjustment of Status packet ‚Äì used by {{us.data.application.name || "Applicant"}} to apply for lawful permanent resident status (commonly known as Green Card)

    How to edit my form entries?

    Option 1: Edit your answers in the Applicant Info or Sponsor Info steps, and the forms will be automatically updated here.

    Option 2: If Option 1 does not fix the issue, then you can edit form entries directly when you open a form in the Greenbroad PDF Viewer. Only use this option if Option 1 does not work, and read the warning message below.

    Warning: Changing your form entries can potentially change your application eligibility, required forms, required supporting documents, filing fees, filing method, and more. Make sure you do the following:

    • If changing form entries using Option 1, make sure to browse through the entire Greenbroad software afterwards for updated instructions.
    • If changing form entries using Option 2, Greenbroad cannot update instructions after you make changes. It is your responsibility to review the relevant USCIS instructions on your own to make sure you are filing correctly.
  2. Review the USCIS instructions for each application form and confirm that all requirements are met correctly.
  3. Review each cover letter and confirm that all the application forms and documents you intend to submit to USCIS are correctly listed on the cover letter. Note: All documents from the Collect Documents step are included in the cover letter, unless the document is marked as "Skipped" or "N/A" by you. So make sure you have marked each item correctly.
