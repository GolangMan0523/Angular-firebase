›Should I submit photocopies or originals?

You may submit legible photocopies of the documents requested, unless the USCIS instructions specifically state that you must submit an original document. USCIS may request an original document at the time of filing or at any time during processing of an application, petition, or request.

If you submit original documents when not required, different forms handle that different, ranging from automatically returning them to you, to returning to you upon request only, to immediately destroying them. Read form specific instructions for details.

›Should copies of supporting documents be single-sided or double sided?
They should be single-sided.

›What if a name has changed?

If either you or your spouse is using a name that is not the same name shown on the relevant documents, you must provide copies of the legal documents reflecting the name change, such as a marriage certificate, adoption decree, or court order.
