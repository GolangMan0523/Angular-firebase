    A cover letter is essentially a table of contents of all the forms and documents contained within an application. A cover letter helps you organize your papers, and also helps USCIS agents during their review.

    Below you can download a cover letter created based on your input in the Applicant Info and Sponsor Info steps, and in the previous Collect Documents section.

Sign, date, etc.

On the printed application forms, please carefully read and complete the following important parts. These parts include signatures, dates, contact information, important statements, declarations, certifications, and contracts with the U.S. government that are required before the application forms can be complete.

Make sure to use a pen with black ink, as this helps USCIS scan your forms correctly.

IMPORTANT: Make sure Forms I-130 and I-130A have been generated for you on the previous page. If it's not the case, contact us before proceeding. USCIS may reject your application if it does not include these forms.

* Greenbroad, LLC is not an interpreter nor a preparer for these application forms. If you did not use an interpreter or a preparer, fill those parts out with "N/A". If you used an interpreter or a preparer, follow the USCIS form instructions to fill out the interpreter and preparer parts.

* Each application form must be properly signed. USCIS will not accept a stamped or typewritten name in place of a signature. A legal guardian may also sign for a mentally incompetent person.

Final check

Since this software is a self-help tool to be used at your specific direction (Greenbroad, LLC is not a law firm and does not have special knowledge of immigration law and procedure), it is your responsibility to make sure your application is filed correctly. Do not proceed to the next step until you are confident with the following statements:

 Do both Applicant and Sponsor agree with all of the following statements?
  1. Applicant and Sponsor have read and understood the relevant USCIS and Department of State Green Card application instructions, application form instructions and other official instructions published by the U.S. government.
  2. Based on the official U.S. government instructions mentioned above, Applicant and Sponsor have decided that they have completed the correct application forms, supporting documents and cover letter(s).
