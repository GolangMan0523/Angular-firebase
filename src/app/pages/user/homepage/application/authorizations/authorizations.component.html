Work and travel while Form I-485 is pending

If you want to work in the U.S. and/or travel outside the U.S. while your Form I-485 is pending, you can apply for work authorization (called “Employment Authorization Document” or “EAD”) by filing Form I-765, and apply for travel authorization (called “Advance Parole Document”) by filing Form I-131. Until recently, these two authorizations were issued together on one card, so they were also known as the “combo card”. But now USCIS actually issues two separate cards, one for work and one for travel.
Greenbroad can help you prepare Forms I-765 and I-131 at no extra cost, and later we will also show you how to file them together with Form I-485.
Will filing I-765 and I-131 impact the green card processing time?
It is not likely to impact the green card processing time. Once received by USCIS, Forms I-765, I-131 and I-485 are separated and processed independently from one another. The green card processing time is governed primarily by I-485’s processing time.
Source: Greenbroad suggestion only, not from USCIS
Does USCIS charge extra filing fees for I-765 and I-131?
No, there is no extra filing fees for I-765 and I-131 when they are filed together with I-485.
Source: USCIS Fee Schedule
Should I file I-765 with I-485 if I’m not sure whether I will work in the U.S. while my I-485 is pending?
It may still be a good idea to file. You don’t need to have a current employment to file Form I-765. Even though you can file I-765 at a later time, its approval can take months. Filing I-765 with I-485 allows you to get the Employment Authorization Document earlier.
Source: Greenbroad suggestion only, not from USCIS
Should I file I-131 with I-485 if I’m not sure whether I will travel outside the U.S. while my I-485 is pending?
It may still be a good idea to file. There is no requirement to provide a detailed itinerary for Form I-131 (it does ask for the date of intended departure and the expected length of trip). Like with I-765, you can file I-131 at a later time, but its approval can take months. Filing I-131 with I-485 allows you to get the Advance Parole Document earlier.
Source: Greenbroad suggestion only, not from USCIS

› What if I have a current EAD or an H-1B visa?

Applying for an EAD with your green card is optional. If you have a current EAD or an H-1B visa, you can still apply for an EAD with your green card application, but you don't have to.

Some applicants may find it desirable to apply for an EAD with their green card application because it is not tied to one particular sponsoring employer like an H-1B visa, or because their current EAD or H-1B visa cannot last through the application period.

Also, in case this is useful, according to page 15 of the Form I-765 instructions, Form I-765 (the form used to apply for employment authorization) can be filed on a later date, separately from Form I-485. So you have some time to decide. If you decide to file Form I-765 on a later date, keep the processing time in mind: Currently, it can take 8 months or more for USCIS to issue an EAD (the actual time it takes varies significantly from case to case), so it is important to plan ahead.

Potential future USCIS fee increase

On Oct 2, 2020, USCIS was going to start charging an extra $550 fee for filing Form I-765, which is the form used to apply for an Employment Authorization Document. However, the fee increase was temporarily blocked by a federal court injunction on Sept 29, 2020. As long as this injunction is in effect, USCIS will not charge the new fee.

Note that this injunction is only temporary and can change at any time without notice. You should double check the USCIS Fee Schedule page right before you file to make sure you are using the correct filing fees.
