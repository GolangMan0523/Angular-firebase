What's the date of filing {{us.data.application.name && us.data.application.name + "'s" || "Applicant's"}} application for adjustment of status?

The date of filing the application for adjustment of status, is the date {{us.data.application.name || "Applicant"}} files Form I-485. Form I-485 is part of the second submission package that you will send to USCIS.

In September and October 2020, applicants can file Form I-485 around two weeks after filing Submission 1. However, the wait time can fluctuate significantly from month to month. For example, as recent as April 2019, applicants had to wait for about 15 months before allowed to file Form I-485. You can find more historical wait time from the past five years by visiting the USCIS Adjustment of Status Filing Charts. For a detailed explanation of the waiting period, read Part 2 of our Complete Application Guide.

Greenbroad Suggestion: For applicants whose current immigration status is expiring soon, it may be challenging to estimate whether they can maintain lawful immigration status until filing Form I-485. If you are unsure about this ineligibility item, we suggest you consult an immigration attorney before proceeding.
