J Visa Two-Year Foreign Residence Requirement

If you are or were a J-1 or J-2 nonimmigrant exchange visitor and are subject to the 2-year foreign residence requirement of INA section 212(e), you may not apply to adjust status unless you have complied with the foreign residence requirement, have been granted a waiver of that requirement, or were issued a favorable waiver recommendation letter from U.S. Department of State (DOS).

Source: USCIS Form I-485 Instructions Page 14

How to find out if I am subject to this requirement?

How to find out if I am subject to this requirement? Not all J-1 visa are subject to the two-year foreign residence requirement. You can determine whether your J-1 is subject by following these rules listed on the Department of State website

If it is not clear from these rules whether you are subject, you can also try:

  • Check with your J-1 program sponsor
  • Check your J-1 documents:
  • On the J-1 visa stamp page in your passport. It may bear the phrase: "Bearer is (or is not) subject to 212(e). Two year rule does/does not apply."
  • In the section in the lower left hand corner of your DS-2019 form labeled "preliminary endorsement."
  • Take the survey available on the U.S. State Department J Visa Waiver Online webpage.

However, please note that none of these methods guarantee 100% certainty. For an official determination of whether you are subject to the two-year foreign residence requirement, you need to request an Advisory Opinion form the U.S. State Department. You can learn more about how to obtain an Advisory Opinion here.
